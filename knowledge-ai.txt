3.2 Hydraulic Power Packs — Overview												
												
Self-contained fluid power generation units: prime mover (electric motor) → pump → reservoir → filtration → manifold logic → cooling.												
												
General specification range:												
												
Parameter	Range											
Motor power	0.5 HP–200+ HP (IE2/IE3/IE4)											
Flow rate	1–300+ LPM											
Pressure	160/210 Bar standard; 350+ Bar heavy-duty											
Reservoir	10L–5,000+ Liters (custom steel/SS fabrication)											
Pump types	Gear, Vane, Radial Piston, Axial Piston											
Cooling	Air-Blast Oil Coolers, Shell & Tube Heat Exchangers											
												
Power pack types:												
												
Type	Power Source	Duty Cycle	Best For									
3-Phase Industrial	415V/440V AC	Continuous 24/7	Factory automation, presses, steel plants									
Single Phase	220V/230V AC	Intermittent	Small workshops, garages, light duty use									
High-Low (dual pump)	415V/440V AC	Continuous; energy-optimized	Presses/forging — fast approach + high-pressure stamping in one cycle, big energy savings									
DC Mini (12V/24V)	Vehicle battery	Intermittent only (S2/S3)	Tipper trucks, tail lifts, mobile dock levelers									
Hand Lever Operated	Manual	Intermittent	Simple lift/lower operations, emergency backup									
With Accumulator	AC/DC	Cyclic	Applications requiring stored energy for rapid actuation or emergency lowering									
With Multiple Solenoid Valve	415V AC	Continuous	Multi-circuit systems, SPMs requiring independent actuator control									
For Lift	415V AC	Continuous	Goods/passenger hydraulic elevator installations									
For Press	415V AC	Continuous	Press applications — hydraulic press braking, stamping, forming									
Custom/Turnkey	Varies	Per spec	SPMs, OEM-specific machines									
												
Sizing formula: Required HP = (Flow in LPM × Pressure in Bar) ÷ 440 (≈85% pump efficiency assumption). Reservoir rule of thumb: 3–5× pump LPM rating for continuous duty.												
												
High-Low logic explained: Large gear pump (fast, low pressure) + small piston pump (slow, high pressure) on one motor. An unloading valve dumps the large pump to tank once a pressure set-point is reached, so the motor never has to deliver both high flow AND high pressure simultaneously — drastically reduces required motor HP and heat generation.												
												
3-Phase vs Single-Phase: 3-phase is mandatory for continuous-duty (100% duty cycle, smooth torque, lower amp draw, no failure-prone capacitors). Single-phase limited to ~5 HP intermittent use (light garages only).												
												
DC power packs: 12V draws far more amperage than 24V for the same work — 24V recommended above ~10-ton loads to protect wiring. Strictly intermittent duty (S2/S3 rated motors — will burn out if run continuously beyond rated minutes).												
												
3.3 Manifold Blocks — Overview												
												
CNC-machined logic blocks (Aluminium 6061-T6 or ST52/EN8 steel/Ductile Iron/SS316) that consolidate directional, relief, and flow-control valves into one zero-leak body, eliminating external pipework.												
												
CETOP / NG size reference (ISO 4401):												
												
Designation	Flow Capacity	Max Pressure	Port Size	Typical Use								
CETOP 3 / NG6	~40 LPM	315 Bar	G3/8"	Small cylinders, light automation								
CETOP 5 / NG10 / "06-size"	~120 LPM (80 cont./120 peak)	315 Bar	G1/2"	Most common — medium industrial automation								
CETOP 7 / NG16 / "10-size"	~250 LPM (200 cont./250 peak)	315 Bar	G3/4"	Heavy presses, large-bore cylinders								
CETOP 8 / NG22	400+ LPM	315 Bar	G1"	Very high-flow systems								
												
Note: NG10, CETOP 5, and "06-size" are the SAME physical interface (different regional names — European/French/North American). Same for NG16/CETOP7/"10-size".												
												
Sizing rule: If circuit flow exceeds 80 LPM, use NG16 or larger (undersizing causes destructive fluid velocity/heat). Formula: Flow (LPM) = Bore² × π/4 × Piston Speed × 60,000 (with bore/speed in metres, m/s).												
												
Block types:												
												
CETOP subplates — standardized single/sandwich/multi-station bodies for universal valve compatibility (Rexroth, Parker, Vickers, Yuken, Bosch, Atos, Wandfluh all interchangeable on the same CETOP size).												
Multi-Station — 2 to 10 valve stations sharing common P/T galleries; parallel (independent control), series (sequential), or tandem-centre (energy-saving idle).												
Custom — bespoke, cartridge-valve-based (Sun Hydraulics/HydraForce/Bosch/Parker/Bucher), for space-constrained or high-logic-density SPMs. 3–6 week lead time vs 1–2 weeks for standard.												
10/06-size (NG16/NG10) — dedicated size-specific product lines.												
												
Material/pressure guide: Aluminium ≤210 Bar; ST52 Steel/Ductile Iron ≤315 Bar (up to 350+ in custom); EN24T alloy steel up to 700 Bar (UHP); SS316L for marine/corrosive ≤250 Bar.

12. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (use these verbatim where relevant)												
												
Q: Custom cylinders possible? Yes — primary focus. Can work from customer CAD or design from operational parameters (load, pressure, environment, cycle).												
												
Q: Max bore/stroke? Standard catalog: 40–300mm bore, up to 3,000mm stroke. Custom: bores exceeding 400mm, strokes to 8,000mm+.												
												
Q: Materials used? Cold-drawn ST52/E355 seamless tube, EN8/EN19/EN24 rods, imported-equivalent polyurethane/PTFE seals (Viton for high-temp).												
												
Q: Is CETOP 5 = NG10 = "06-size"? Yes, identical ISO 4401-05 interface — fully interchangeable across brands.												
												
Q: NG10 vs NG16 — which do I need? Calculate actual flow (bore² × π/4 × velocity × 60,000). Above 80 LPM → NG16. Above ~200 LPM → CETOP 8/NG22.												
												
Q: Tie Rod vs Welded cylinder? Tie Rod = indoor, ≤210 Bar, fast field service. Welded = outdoor/shock-load/>210 Bar, compact, harder to service (needs lathe to cut weld for seal access).												
												
Q: Repair an existing/imported cylinder with no part number? Yes — reverse engineering service: dismantle, measure, manufacture exact or improved replacement.												
												
Q: 3-phase power pack on single-phase supply? No — requires VFD or phase converter; generally only viable for smaller HP units.												
												
Q: Why is my single-phase power pack overheating? Single-phase motors are intermittent-duty by design; for continuous automation, 3-phase is mandatory.												
												
Q: Counterbalance valve vs POCV for lifting? Counterbalance valve is the safety standard for goods/personnel lifts — provides load-holding independent of pilot signal AND over-running load protection (POCV alone does not protect against runaway descent).												
												
Q: Why does my core-pull cylinder drift during injection? Injection back-pressure overcoming an inadequate load-holding valve — fix is a pilot-operated check valve (POCV) at the cylinder's A port, pilot-operated from main system pressure.												
												
Q: Standard seal for hot/abrasive (steel plant) environments? Upgrade to Viton (FKM) rod seals/wipers; for furnace-adjacent equipment use heat-resistant alloys + Viton/Kalrez.												
												
Q: Do you provide field installation/PLC integration? Yes — field engineers handle on-site tubing, commissioning, sensor wiring, and PID tuning alongside the customer's PLC programmers.												
												
Q: Ship outside Gujarat/India? Yes — PAN-India and export, with full documentation and heavy-duty wooden crating.												
												
Q: Do you supply hard chrome plated rods and honed tubes separately? Yes — we supply hard chrome plated rods, induction hardened rods, and honed tubes as standalone products, in addition to using them in our own cylinder manufacturing.												
												
Q: Can you manufacture for car parking / passenger lift / goods lift applications? Yes — we manufacture dedicated hydraulic cylinders for car parking systems, passenger lifts (to IS 14665), goods lifts, and scissor tables.
