# 3. What Are CETOP Manifold Blocks?

**CETOP** (Comité Européen des Transmissions Oléohydrauliques et Pneumatiques) is the European standards body that established the ISO 4401 mounting interface for hydraulic directional control valves. A **CETOP manifold block** — also called a subplate or valve base — is a precision-machined mounting platform that provides:

1.  **A standardized bolt pattern** for mounting any CETOP-compliant directional valve
2.  **Internal fluid galleries** connecting the P (Pressure), T (Tank), A, and B work ports of the valve to the external system ports on the subplate body
3.  **Port threads** (BSPP, NPT, or SAE) for connecting the subplate to the wider hydraulic circuit

Because the mounting pattern is internationally standardized, an OEM can specify a Honeywell-manufactured CETOP 5 subplate and mount any CETOP 5 valve from any brand onto it without modification — a critical advantage for procurement flexibility and long-term spares availability.

# 4. CETOP Size Comparison Table

Understanding the correct CETOP size for your system flow requirement is fundamental to avoiding pressure drops.

| CETOP Standard | ISO 4401 Size | Nominal Flow | Typical Working Pressure | Port Thread Size | Common Applications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CETOP 3** | NG6 | Up to 40 LPM | Up to 315 Bar | G3/8" BSPP | Small cylinders, light automation |
| **CETOP 5** | NG10 | Up to 120 LPM | Up to 315 Bar | G1/2" BSPP | Medium industrial automation |
| **CETOP 7** | NG16 | Up to 250 LPM | Up to 315 Bar | G3/4" BSPP | Heavy presses, large actuators |
| **CETOP 8** | NG22 | Up to 400+ LPM | Up to 315 Bar | G1" BSPP | Very high-flow industrial systems |

*A mismatched CETOP size (e.g., CETOP 3 on a 80 LPM circuit) causes catastrophic fluid velocity in the internal galleries, generating heat and destroying the valve. Always size by flow rate, not by convenience.*

# 5. Standard Subplate Configurations

We manufacture CETOP subplates in the following standard body configurations:

### A. Inline (In-Line) Subplates
The A and B work ports exit from the same face as the system P and T ports. This is the most common configuration for simple, single-cylinder circuits.

### B. Subbase (Sandwich) Subplates
A taller body design intended to allow additional modular sandwich plates (pressure-reducing, flow-control, or check-valve plates) to be stacked between the subbase and the directional valve. This provides additional circuit flexibility without adding external pipework.

### C. Multiple-Station Subplates
A single elongated body machined to accept 2, 3, 4, or more CETOP valves in a parallel arrangement, sharing common P and T galleries while providing individual A and B work ports per station. This is the entry point to our [Multi-Station Manifold Block](/manifold-blocks/multi-station-manifold-blocks/) range.

# 6. Material & Machining Specifications

Our CETOP subplates are manufactured to guarantee zero-leak valve seating under cyclic operating conditions.

*   **Standard Material (Up to 210 Bar):** Aluminium Alloy 6061-T6. Lightweight, excellent machinability, anodized for corrosion resistance.
*   **High-Pressure Material (Up to 315 Bar):** ST52 Carbon Steel or EN8 Steel. Full zinc-plating or black oxide finish.
*   **Mounting Face Flatness:** Machined to Ra 0.8 µm or better on the valve mounting face to ensure uniform O-ring compression and zero-leak seating.
*   **Port Tolerances:** Internal galleries finished to H8 tolerance. Port threads chased and gauged to BS21 (BSPP) or ANSI B1.20.1 (NPT) standards.
*   **Testing:** 100% hydrostatically leak-tested at 1.5x rated working pressure before despatch.

# 13. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the difference between CETOP 3 and CETOP 5?**
**A:** The primary difference is the flow capacity. CETOP 3 (NG6) is rated to approximately 40 LPM and uses G3/8" ports. CETOP 5 (NG10) is rated to approximately 120 LPM and uses G1/2" ports. Choosing the wrong size will cause excessive fluid velocity and heat generation. Always size by your circuit's actual flow rate.

**Q: Can you machine additional ports or cavities into a standard CETOP subplate?**
**A:** Yes. We routinely machine additional pilot ports, gauge ports (G1/4"), drain ports, and cartridge valve cavities into standard CETOP subplates to suit specific circuit requirements. Provide your drawing or describe your requirements and we will quote accordingly.

**Q: Do you supply CETOP subplates in stainless steel for corrosive environments?**
**A:** Yes. For marine, chemical, and food-processing applications, we can machine CETOP subplates in SS316L stainless steel. Please note this is a special-order item with longer lead times.

**Q: What is the minimum order quantity for CETOP subplates?**
**A:** We supply from single pieces for prototyping up to high-volume OEM batch runs. There is no minimum order quantity for standard configurations.
