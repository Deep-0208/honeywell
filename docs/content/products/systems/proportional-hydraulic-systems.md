# 3. What Are Proportional Hydraulic Systems?

Standard [Industrial Hydraulic Systems](/hydraulic-systems/industrial-hydraulic-systems/) operate using "bang-bang" solenoid valves. The valve is either completely open or completely closed. While effective for heavy pressing or moving loads from point A to point B, this standard "open loop" logic cannot achieve extreme precision.

When a testing laboratory needs to simulate a dynamic airplane wing load, or a machine builder needs to stop a 10-ton hydraulic cylinder within 0.01 millimeters of a target, they require a **Proportional Hydraulic System**.

Also known as **Electro-Hydraulic or Servo-Hydraulic Systems**, these advanced circuits utilize highly sensitive proportional or servo valves. Instead of just opening or closing, these valves open *proportionally* based on an analog voltage or current signal from a computer (e.g., 0-10V or 4-20mA). By combining these infinite-position valves with high-speed electronic sensors, we create a "Closed Loop" system where the hydraulic machine constantly "talks" to the PLC, adjusting its own flow and pressure hundreds of times per second to achieve absolute perfection.

# 5. How Proportional Hydraulic Systems Work

The architecture of a closed-loop system is an intricate dance between electrical physics and fluid dynamics. 

### A. The Nerves (High-Resolution Sensors)
To control a machine, the computer must know exactly what the machine is doing in real-time. We integrate Linear Variable Differential Transformers (LVDTs) directly inside the [Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/) to measure the exact rod position. We install high-frequency digital pressure transducers in the [Manifold Blocks](/manifold-blocks/) to monitor hydraulic force, and utilize advanced load cells to measure physical mechanical resistance.

### B. The Brain (PLC Logic & PID Control)
The master Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) continuously reads the sensor data. It compares the cylinder's *actual* position to the *desired* position programmed by the operator. If there is a discrepancy (an "error"), the PLC utilizes a highly complex mathematical algorithm called a PID Loop (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) to calculate exactly how much fluid is needed to correct the error.

### C. The Heart (Servo Valves & Proportional Valves)
The PLC sends a milliamp analog signal to the proportional valve. The valve's magnetic spool shifts by a microscopic amount—perhaps just a fraction of a millimeter—allowing the exact required volume of fluid to enter the cylinder. Because the sensors are reading data continuously, the valve adjusts continuously, creating a perfectly smooth, perfectly accurate mechanical movement.

# 6. Precision Motion Control Capabilities

Our custom servo-hydraulic systems offer three distinct axes of absolute control:

### A. Position Control
Stopping a heavy moving load exactly where it needs to be. By utilizing custom deceleration ramping profiles within the PLC, we can move a massive forging die rapidly, slow it down smoothly right before impact, and stop it within micrometers of the target, eliminating mechanical shock and drastically extending the lifespan of the machine frame.

### B. Speed (Velocity) Control
Maintaining a perfectly constant speed regardless of the load. If a [3-Phase Hydraulic Power Pack](/hydraulic-power-packs/3-phase-hydraulic-power-packs/) pushes fluid into a standard cylinder and the load suddenly gets heavier, the cylinder will slow down. In a closed-loop system, the velocity sensors instantly detect the slowdown, and the proportional valve instantly opens wider to compensate, ensuring a perfectly constant velocity profile.

### C. Force (Pressure) Control
Simulating exact physical resistance. In material testing, a machine must apply exactly 50 kN of force to a steel sample, neither a Newton more nor a Newton less. Our closed-loop pressure systems monitor the load cell feedback and rapidly adjust proportional pressure-relief valves to maintain a flawless force curve.

# 9. Engineering Considerations (Designing Closed-Loop Circuits)

Designing a servo-hydraulic system requires our [Custom Hydraulic System Design](/services/custom-hydraulic-system-design/) architects to bridge mechanical engineering with advanced computer science.

### A. Control Stability & Resonance
If a PID loop is not tuned correctly, a proportional system will "hunt" for the target, causing the heavy cylinder to vibrate violently. We perform advanced mathematical bode-plot analysis to ensure the natural frequency of the hydraulic oil column does not conflict with the mass of the payload, ensuring absolute system stability.

### B. Signal Conditioning & Electrical Noise
Analog signals (0-10V or 4-20mA) are highly susceptible to electromagnetic interference (EMI) from the factory floor. If a sensor signal is corrupted, the machine will crash. We specify heavily shielded sensor cabling, galvanic isolators, and dedicated ground loops to guarantee pristine signal transmission to the PLC.

### C. Extreme Filtration (NAS Class 4)
A standard directional valve can tolerate a small amount of dirt. A high-performance servo valve will seize immediately if a microscopic particle enters its flapper-nozzle assembly. Our proportional systems mandate absolute 3-micron glass-fiber filtration, offline kidney-loop conditioning, and continuous digital particle counters to maintain aerospace-grade fluid cleanliness.

# 17. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Why does my proportional hydraulic cylinder vibrate violently when it reaches the target?**
**A:** This is almost always caused by a poorly tuned PID loop in the PLC, specifically a "Proportional Gain" that is set too high. The computer is over-correcting the error, pushing the cylinder past the target, then violently pulling it back, creating a phenomenon called "hunting." Our engineers specialize in tuning these loops for absolute stability.

**Q: What is the difference between a proportional valve and a servo valve?**
**A:** Both provide infinite positioning. However, a true servo valve is built with extreme mechanical precision (often using a flapper-nozzle design) allowing for incredibly fast frequency response times (often <10 milliseconds). Servo valves are used for high-speed dynamic testing. Proportional valves are slightly slower but much more robust against dirt, making them ideal for heavy industrial precision (like a deep drawing press).

**Q: Can you upgrade our standard hydraulic press to a proportional system?**
**A:** Yes. Through our [Hydraulic Retrofitting](/services/hydraulic-retrofitting/) division, we can install LVDT position sensors into your existing cylinders, replace the standard manifold with a proportional logic block, and integrate a new PLC to transform your basic press into a precision forming machine.

**Q: Why do proportional systems require such extreme oil filtration?**
**A:** A standard directional valve might have internal clearances of 20 microns. A high-performance servo valve operates with internal clearances of 2 to 3 microns. If standard hydraulic oil is used, microscopic dirt particles will instantly jam the servo valve spool, causing catastrophic machine failure. Absolute 3-micron offline filtration is mandatory.
