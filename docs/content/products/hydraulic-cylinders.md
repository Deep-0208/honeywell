# 4. Types of Hydraulic Cylinders

We manufacture a comprehensive range of hydraulic cylinder types, each engineered for specific industrial applications and mounting requirements. 

## [Double Acting Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/double-acting/)
Double acting cylinders are the backbone of modern industrial automation. Unlike single acting designs, they utilize hydraulic pressure to provide force in both the extend and retract strokes. 

## [Single Acting Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/single-acting/)
Single acting cylinders apply hydraulic force in only one direction (usually extension), relying on an external mechanical force, gravity, or an internal spring to return the piston to its original position. 

## [Flange Mounting Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/flange-mounting/)
Flange mounted cylinders feature a solid, bolted flange interface—either on the front (rod end) or rear (cap end) of the cylinder body, providing excellent centerline force transfer.

## [Clevis Mounting Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/clevis-mounting/)
When an application requires the cylinder to pivot in a curved arc during its stroke, clevis mounting cylinders are the optimal solution. 

## [Tie Rod Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/tie-rod/)
Tie rod cylinders are the standard in industrial manufacturing, utilizing high-strength threaded steel rods to hold the end caps securely to the cylinder barrel. 

## [Telescopic Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/telescopic/)
For applications requiring an exceptionally long stroke from a highly compact retracted length, telescopic hydraulic cylinders are the definitive answer.
