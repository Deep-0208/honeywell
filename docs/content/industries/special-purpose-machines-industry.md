# 3. Why SPMs Demand Custom Hydraulic Engineering

A standard hydraulic power pack from a catalogue can move a cylinder. But an SPM requires hydraulics that:

*   **Execute a precise, multi-step sequence** — Cylinder A clamps, then cylinder B advances, then pressure builds to a set-point, then cylinder C punches, then all retract in reverse order — all reliably and repeatably.
*   **Control simultaneous motion** — Multiple cylinders extending at identical speeds, or at specifically different speeds, all from one power source without interfering with each other.
*   **Interface with the machine PLC** — Every solenoid valve fires exactly when the PLC commands it, and the hydraulic system's pressure and position sensors report back to the PLC with complete fidelity.
*   **Fit within the machine frame** — SPM hydraulic systems often have a defined envelope within the machine frame that requires compact manifold design and creative piping layouts.
*   **Deliver consistent results on part #1,000,000** — An SPM is often rated for a production life of 10+ years. The hydraulic system must maintain its performance specifications throughout that service life.

# 4. SPM Hydraulic System Types We Design

### Multi-Cylinder Clamping Systems
Hydraulic clamping systems for fixture plates holding complex components during machining. We design and supply the complete clamping manifold, individual clamp cylinders with load-holding POCVs, and the 3-phase power unit — all sized and sequenced for the SPM's specific clamping force and sequence requirements.

### Pressing & Force-Control Systems
SPMs for pressing bearings, bush interference fits, and assembly force verification use hydraulic cylinders with integrated force monitoring (load cells) and closed-loop force control. We design these systems with position-vs-force graph monitoring capability to allow real-time quality verification of every pressed part.

### Transfer & Indexing Machine Hydraulics
Hydraulic actuators for SPM transfer slides, rotary indexing tables, and pick-and-place mechanisms. These typically require high-speed, short-stroke cylinders with very consistent, repeatable positioning — often achieved through mechanical hard-stops combined with proportional deceleration valves.

### Test Rig Hydraulic Systems
SPMs configured as production test rigs — testing the hydraulic function of valves, cylinders, or complete sub-assemblies — require hydraulic systems capable of generating precise, repeatable test pressures and flows. We design [Proportional Hydraulic Systems](/hydraulic-systems/proportional-hydraulic-systems/) for test rig applications requiring data-acquisition-grade measurement accuracy.

# 7. Case Study

**[PLACEHOLDER PROJECT SPOTLIGHT]**
**Project:** 10-Axis Hydraulic SPM for Automotive Transmission Housing Machining
**Client:** [PLACEHOLDER CLIENT], SPM Builder, Vadodara
**Challenge:** The SPM required 10 hydraulic clamps to hold an aluminium automatic transmission housing during a 5-axis CNC machining operation. All 10 clamps had to be sequenced to apply within 4 seconds, each at a different specified clamping force (from 2 kN to 15 kN), and all had to load-hold for up to 45 minutes during the machining cycle.
**Solution:** We designed a custom 10-station cartridge manifold with individual Sun Hydraulics pressure-reducing cartridges per station (allowing different pressures from one supply) and pilot-operated check valve load-holding per station. Complete system designed, assembled, and FAT-tested before delivery.
**Outcome:** All 10 clamps sequence within 3.2 seconds. Individual clamping forces verified within ±2.5% of target. No clamping force drift has been recorded during 45-minute machining cycles. The SPM passed the automotive customer's final acceptance test on first attempt.

# 9. FAQ

**Q: We are building our first SPM and have limited hydraulic design experience. Can you design the entire system?**
**A:** Yes. Our [Custom Hydraulic System Design](/services/custom-hydraulic-system-design/) service is specifically designed for exactly this situation. You tell us what each hydraulic actuator must do, and we engineer the complete fluid power system — calculating forces, designing the circuit, manufacturing all components, and commissioning the complete system in your machine frame.

**Q: How do you handle sequence valve logic for complex multi-step SPM cycles?**
**A:** For complex sequences, we use a combination of PLC-controlled solenoid valves (for operator-controllable sequencing) and hydraulic sequence valves or pressure-controlled directional valves (for automatic pressure-dependent sequencing). The choice depends on the required flexibility and the PLC's available I/O.

**Q: Can you provide the hydraulic system for an SPM that we will manufacture in series (10+ units per year)?**
**A:** Yes. Once the first-off hydraulic system is tested and approved, we establish an OEM manufacturing program through our [OEM Hydraulic Manufacturing](/services/oem-hydraulic-manufacturing/) division to supply the series-production hydraulic sub-assemblies to your assembly schedule.
