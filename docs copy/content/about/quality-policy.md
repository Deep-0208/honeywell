# 4. The Five Pillars of Our Quality Commitment

### Pillar 1: Physics-Based Design (Not Catalogue-Based Copying)
Quality begins at the design phase. We calculate — not assume — the required wall thickness of every cylinder, the minimum gallery bore diameter of every manifold, and the required motor kilowatt of every power unit. Undersized components look identical to correctly specified ones, and only fail under load, in service, at the worst possible time.

Our minimum design standards:
*   Hydraulic cylinders: Minimum **4:1 safety factor** on all pressure-bearing walls
*   Manifold blocks: Maximum **4.5 m/s** fluid velocity in all galleries at rated flow
*   Power units: Minimum **3× pump flow rate** reservoir capacity for thermal stability

### Pillar 2: Material Traceability
Every kilogram of steel, every aluminium billet, and every seal kit used in our production carries a material certificate or a supplier quality certificate. Raw material is received into our stores only after its certificate is verified against the purchase order specification. Critical pressure-bearing components are laser-engraved with a heat code that links to the raw material certificate — providing full traceability from finished component back to the steel mill.

### Pillar 3: In-Process Dimensional Inspection
We do not rely on final inspection alone to catch manufacturing errors. Our process includes:
*   **First-Off Inspection:** Every new job's first component is fully measured before production run begins
*   **In-Process Spot Checks:** Random dimensional verification at key machining stages
*   **100% Critical Feature Inspection:** Thread gauging on all ports, bore gauge on all cylinder barrels, concentricity check on all rods

### Pillar 4: 100% Hydrostatic Pressure Testing
**Every single pressure-bearing component we manufacture is hydrostatically tested at 1.5× its rated working pressure before despatch.** No exceptions. A hydrostatic test certificate identifying the component, its rated pressure, the test pressure applied, and the test result (pass/fail) accompanies every shipment.

This is the non-negotiable foundation of our quality system. Hydrostatic testing is the only reliable method of verifying a hydraulic component's structural integrity — dimensional inspection alone cannot detect weld defects, porosity, or material flaws that will cause failure under pressure.

### Pillar 5: Continuous Improvement
Our production team reviews all warranty claims, field failures, and client complaints monthly. Root cause analyses are performed for every warranty claim. Corrective actions are implemented in the production process before the next production run. We track our warranty claim rate as our primary quality performance metric.

2. We will respond within [PLACEHOLDER_HOURS] working hours with a preliminary assessment.
3. If the failure is attributable to a manufacturing defect (as determined by our technical team's inspection of the returned component), we will replace or repair the component at our cost with priority lead time.

### Exclusions
Our warranty does not cover failures attributable to: (a) system contamination above the component's specified ISO 4406 cleanliness limit, (b) operation beyond the component's rated pressure or temperature, (c) physical damage from external impact or mishandling, or (d) use of incompatible hydraulic fluids.
