# 3. Plastic Processing Hydraulic Applications

### Extrusion Machine Screen Changers
Continuous extrusion lines use hydraulic cylinders to slide a filter screen pack across the melt stream for screen changes without stopping production. These cylinders require very high force in a very compact stroke at low speed — demanding extreme rigidity and precision positioning.

### Die Head & Wall Thickness Controllers
Blown film and pipe extrusion die heads use an array of small hydraulic actuators (pushers) arranged radially around the die to adjust annular gap thickness and compensate for eccentricity in the extrudate. These are highly precision proportional hydraulic systems requiring ±0.01mm positioning accuracy on small-bore cylinders.

### Thermoforming Machine Clamp & Platen Drive
Thermoforming machines use hydraulic cylinders to drive the clamping frame that holds the heated plastic sheet, and to drive the forming platen into the mould. These circuits require smooth, controlled velocity to prevent the hot sheet from tearing during clamping contact.

### Blow Moulding Clamp Systems
Hydraulic clamp systems for blow moulding machines that must generate high clamping force rapidly to close around the parison, hold during blowing, and retract cleanly for part ejection — typically in cycles of 5–15 seconds continuously.

### Hydraulic Die Head Adjustment
Pipe and profile extrusion lines use hydraulic actuators to adjust die lip opening for online wall thickness correction. These are precision, slow-speed applications requiring very fine proportional flow control.

# 4. Key Differentiation from Injection Moulding Hydraulics

While [injection moulding](/industries/injection-moulding/) focuses on fast, high-force clamping and core-pull sequencing, plastic processing hydraulics is characterized by:

| Factor | Injection Moulding | Plastic Processing (Extrusion/Thermoforming) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Force** | High (clamping tonnage) | Medium to high (screen change, clamping) |
| **Primary Accuracy Need** | Positional (core pull location) | Velocity (smooth clamp approach) |
| **Cycle Time** | Seconds (discrete) | Continuous or very long cycle |
| **Temperature** | Standard ambient | Often elevated (near extruder heat) |
| **Contamination** | Plastic dust | Polymer off-gas condensate |

# 9. FAQ

**Q: What contamination risks do plastic processing environments pose to hydraulic systems?**
**A:** Polymer off-gases from extruders can condense on exposed metal surfaces and enter reservoir breathers, contaminating the hydraulic oil with polymer particles that foul filter elements rapidly. We recommend sealed desiccant breathers and elevated-position reservoir placement to minimize off-gas exposure. We also recommend more frequent oil analysis than standard industrial practice.

**Q: Can you supply hydraulic screen-change cylinders to match our existing extruder dimensions?**
**A:** Yes. Screen changer cylinders are typically custom-dimensioned for the specific extruder. Provide us with the bore, stroke, maximum force requirement, and mounting flange dimensions, and we will manufacture a matching replacement.
