# 3. Understanding the NG10 / 06 Designation

The **NG10** and **06-Size** designations both refer to the same physical valve interface, governed by **ISO 4401** (CETOP 5). The dual naming convention exists because different regions use different terminology:

*   **NG10:** European designation used by Rexroth, Atos, and Wandfluh. "NG" = Nenngrüße (Nominal Size in German).
*   **CETOP 5:** French standards body designation. Numerically refers to the standardized mounting interface.
*   **06-Size:** North American designation used by Parker, Vickers, and Eaton/Danfoss.

**All three designations are physically identical.** A Parker D3W "06-size" directional valve will mount directly onto a Honeywell CETOP 5 (NG10) manifold block — same bolt pattern, same port locations, same O-ring groove sizes.

# 4. NG10 Performance Envelope

Understanding the operating limits of the NG10 interface prevents undersizing (which causes dangerous heat generation) or oversizing (which wastes capital).

| Parameter | NG10 (CETOP 5 / 06-Size) Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ISO Standard** | ISO 4401-05-05-0-05 |
| **Nominal Flow Capacity** | 80 LPM (continuous), 120 LPM (peak) |
| **Maximum Working Pressure** | 315 Bar (in steel/iron blocks) |
| **Mounting Bolt Pattern** | 4 × M6 bolts, ISO 4401-05 spacing |
| **P Port (Pressure In)** | G 1/2" BSPP (or SAE-8) |
| **T Port (Tank Return)** | G 1/2" BSPP (or SAE-8) |
| **A & B Work Ports** | G 3/8" BSPP (or SAE-6) |
| **Valve Seal Size** | D-ring or O-ring to ISO 4401-05 specification |

*Attempting to run 200 LPM through an NG10 block will create fluid velocity exceeding 6 m/s in the internal galleries, generating severe heat and turbulence that destroys the directional valve internals.*

# 5. NG10 Block Product Range

We manufacture the full NG10 product family:

### A. Single-Station NG10 Subplates
Standard in-line subplates in Aluminium (≤210 Bar) and ST52 Steel (≤315 Bar). Available with G1/2" BSPP, SAE-8 O-Ring Boss, or SAE Code 61 flange ports in P and T positions.

### B. NG10 Multi-Station Rails
2-station to 8-station parallel rail manifolds sharing a common P and T gallery. Machined from a single billet of ST52 steel for maximum structural integrity. Available with integrated gauge ports and optional POCV cartridge provisions per station.

### C. NG10 Sandwich (Subbase) Plates
Extended subbase bodies designed to stack one or more modular sandwich plates (flow control, pressure reducing, check valve plates) between the subbase and the directional valve, providing circuit modification capability without external pipework.

### D. NG10 Custom Integration Blocks
Purpose-machined NG10-compatible blocks incorporating additional logic cavities for specific circuit functions — such as combined CETOP 5 directional valves plus Sun Hydraulics cartridge-type relief and sequence valves in a single monolithic block.

# 6. NG10 vs. NG6 and NG16: Choosing the Right Size

| Design Consideration | NG6 (CETOP 3) | **NG10 (CETOP 5) — THIS PAGE** | NG16 (CETOP 7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Max Flow** | 40 LPM | 120 LPM | 250 LPM |
| **Typical Cylinder Bore** | Up to 63mm | 63mm to 160mm | 100mm to 250mm |
| **Block Size (Single Station)**| Very compact | Medium (industry standard) | Large |
| **Availability** | High | **Highest (most common)** | Medium |
| **Port Size** | G3/8" | G1/2" | G3/4" |

*If you are in doubt between NG10 and NG16, calculate your actual circuit flow: Cylinder Bore² × π/4 × Piston Speed = Flow Rate (LPM). Match this to the NG size capacity.*

# 7. Case Study

**[PLACEHOLDER PROJECT SPOTLIGHT]**
**Project:** NG10 4-Station Manifold Block for Injection Moulding Core-Pull System
**Client:** [PLACEHOLDER CLIENT], Plastic Injection Mould Manufacturer
**Location:** Sachin GIDC, Surat

**The Challenge:**
A mould manufacturer building a complex 8-cavity injection mould needed to integrate a 4-axis hydraulic core-pull system into the mould itself. The available space inside the mould base was critically limited. The four core-pull cylinders were each 100mm bore requiring approximately 65 LPM at full extension speed. Their supplier was offering four individual NG10 subplates that, when connected with external piping, would not fit in the mould base.

**The Solution:**
We designed a compact 4-station NG10 parallel manifold block with all four valve stations on one face, oriented to fit within the mould base cavity dimensions. All P and T connections were internalized, with only four individual A/B work port pairs and the main P/T inlet ports accessible externally. Block height was minimized by using low-profile solenoid valves specified by the client.

**The Outcome:**
The complete 4-axis core-pull manifold assembly fit within the mould base with 12mm to spare. The mould achieved a 6.2-second cycle time — 0.8 seconds faster than the competitor's external-pipework solution — because the eliminated external pipework reduced fluid dead-volume and response lag.

# 11. FAQ

**Q: Is CETOP 5, NG10, and 06-Size all the same thing?**
**A:** Yes, completely. They are three different regional naming conventions for the identical ISO 4401-05 valve mounting interface. Any valve labelled with any of these three designations will mount on our block interchangeably.

**Q: What is the correct internal gallery bore size for an NG10 block at 80 LPM?**
**A:** To maintain fluid velocity below 6 m/s at 80 LPM, the minimum internal gallery bore diameter is 17mm for the P and T galleries. Our standard NG10 blocks use 18mm galleries for the P and T runs, providing a comfortable margin.

**Q: Do you supply NG10 blocks for proportional valve mounting?**
**A:** Yes. Proportional directional valves (such as the Rexroth 4WRPEH or Parker EW series) use the same NG10 mounting interface. The only difference is that proportional valve mounting faces require a finer surface finish (Ra 0.4 µm or better) to ensure the valve's internal spool seats flush. We specify this finish for all proportional-rated blocks.
