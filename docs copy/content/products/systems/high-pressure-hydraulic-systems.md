# 3. What Are High Pressure Hydraulic Systems?

Standard industrial hydraulic circuits operate in the 140–210 Bar range. A **High Pressure Hydraulic System** is defined as any circuit operating continuously above 315 Bar, with ultra-high-pressure (UHP) systems reaching 700 Bar (10,000 PSI) and beyond.

At these extreme pressure levels, the physics of the system change fundamentally. Standard gear pumps cannot survive. Standard manifold blocks made from aluminium will fail catastrophically. Standard O-ring seals will extrude and rupture. Achieving reliable, sustained operation at 700 Bar demands a completely different engineering philosophy — specialised radial or axial piston pumps, solid-steel manifold architecture, and metallic face-seal fittings throughout.

# 5. High Pressure System Architecture

Engineering a system that reliably and safely sustains 700 Bar requires a fundamentally different component selection at every layer:

### A. Ultra-High-Pressure Pumps
Standard gear pumps are limited to approximately 280 Bar. For UHP service, we specify:
- **Radial Piston Pumps:** Extremely low pulsation, capable of 700 Bar continuous service with high volumetric efficiency.
- **Axial Piston Pumps (Swash-Plate):** Variable displacement designs for pressure-compensated operation, ideal for systems requiring large force over a variable stroke at energy efficiency.
- **Intensifier Circuits:** For applications requiring 700 Bar at very low flow rates, we design air-over-oil or hydraulic-over-hydraulic intensifier circuits to step up standard system pressure.

### B. Heavy-Wall Steel Manifolds
At 700 Bar, aluminium will deform. We machine our UHP manifold blocks exclusively from EN24T (40CrNiMo) alloy steel or ST52 carbon steel, with wall thicknesses and bore diameters precisely calculated to withstand a minimum 2x burst safety factor. All cavities are machined with H7 tolerance fits. We supply full material certificates for every block.

### C. Metallic Face-Seal (SAE O-Ring Face Seal) Connections
Tapered pipe threads (BSPT/NPT) are categorically unsafe above 400 Bar as they rely on thread deformation for sealing. All our UHP systems use SAE Code 61/62 four-bolt flanges and O-Ring Face Seal (ORFS) fittings, which provide a positive metallic seal against a precision-machined face — the only reliable connection technology for sustained UHP service.

### D. High-Pressure Cylinders
We manufacture purpose-built, thick-wall [Welded Hydraulic Cylinders](/hydraulic-cylinders/welded-hydraulic-cylinders/) for UHP service. The cylinder barrel wall thickness, piston rod diameter, and end-cap thread engagement are all calculated using ASME pressure vessel code methodology to ensure structural safety at working pressure plus a mandated safety factor.

# 6. Infrastructure Lifting & Bridge Jacking

One of the most demanding applications for high pressure hydraulics is **synchronized bridge jacking** for bearing replacement or structural realignment.

### The Engineering Challenge
A highway bridge superstructure may weigh 5,000 to 50,000 tonnes. The engineer must lift this massive, complex structure by precisely 50mm — uniformly across 12, 24, or even 48 lift points simultaneously — without inducing any differential settlement that would crack the structure. This requires:
- Absolute synchronization of all jack cylinders (to within ±0.5mm)
- Continuous real-time load monitoring at each jack point
- Fail-safe lock-nut mechanisms to prevent catastrophic drop in event of a hydraulic failure

We design custom synchronized lifting systems using high-pressure flat-jack cylinders, a centralized UHP manifold with individual proportional flow controls per jack point, and a master PLC monitoring load cells at every lift point.

# 12. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the maximum pressure your hydraulic systems can achieve?**
**A:** We engineer systems up to 700 Bar (10,000 PSI) using radial piston pumps or hydraulic intensifier circuits. For standard heavy-duty pressing applications, our systems typically operate in the 315–420 Bar range using axial piston variable-displacement pumps.

**Q: Why can't standard industrial manifold blocks be used at 700 Bar?**
**A:** Standard aluminium manifold blocks are rated to a maximum of 210 Bar. At higher pressures, the aluminium will plastically deform around the O-ring seal cavities, causing catastrophic internal and external leaks. For pressures above 315 Bar, we exclusively use dense ST52 carbon steel or EN24T alloy steel with precision-calculated wall thicknesses.

**Q: Do you provide third-party witnessed pressure testing?**
**A:** Yes. We can arrange for a third-party inspection agency (such as Bureau Veritas, SGS, or DNV) to witness and certify our factory hydrostatic pressure tests, providing independent validation certificates suitable for client audits or government infrastructure project requirements.

**Q: Can you add a safety lock mechanism to a high-pressure jack system?**
**A:** Absolutely. For all infrastructure lifting applications, we integrate mechanical lock-nut collars on the cylinder rods and pilot-operated check valves directly at the cylinder port. This creates a fully mechanical and hydraulic dual-redundancy system — the load physically cannot drop even if both the hydraulic hose and the pump fail simultaneously.
